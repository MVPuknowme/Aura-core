Backup connectivity

Designed around fallback planning for ISP outages, grid instability, device failure, and local power disruption. The goal is practical recovery, not unsupported hype.

Local node model

Homes, offices, and community sites may eventually provide compute, storage, connectivity, or proof presence through consent-based onboarding.

Proof-first operations

Every public claim should connect to a proof trail: GitHub commits, health JSON, button routes, pilot records, and transparent changelogs.

Pilot / Demo Only

Lease Your Node

SKYGRID is exploring a model where approved participants can provide compute space, storage, and backup network presence from local devices or sites.

This is not a guaranteed payout program yet. Paid participation requires verified monitoring, contracts, consent, compliance review, and clear operator approval.

  • Device-owner opt-in tracking
  • Solar / backup power readiness
  • Connectivity options: Ethernet, Wi-Fi, cellular, satellite, LoRa, MQTT, and other safe fallbacks
  • Proof packet review before public claims

Launch guardrails

Important: SKYGRID should not claim guaranteed uptime, guaranteed revenue, or active commercial node payouts until monitoring and contracts are verified.

The current public posture is pilot-stage infrastructure development, not a finished financial product.
